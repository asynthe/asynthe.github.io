usage

simply specify disk (you list your disks with lsblk) # ventoy -i <disk>

after that, remember to mount your usb to anywhere on your disk, so you can then copy the .iso file into it. $ mkdir /tmp/usb # mount <disk> /tmp/usb note: mount the partition of your usb which has more space, that is the one to use (name is not just /dev/sda)

now copy the .iso into your usb # cp <file> /tmp/usb
