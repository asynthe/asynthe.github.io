EXTRA

VM tips from The Linux Cast

  • Saved State VMs preserve your vm exactly as it was, then come back to it useful when

    • Host machine has to rebooted
    • Walking away
    • Switching to another VM (saving resources)

    caution: some saved state may not come back like you saved them. happens rarely with distros like Ubuntu / Gnome. caution: coming back there's a chance that the clock may be wrong, make sure to check it out. (can mess with 2FA or SSL, basically, big part of the internet can broke.)

  • clone

very good way to backup your VM, you can also have VM snapshots. clones are your friends.

  • storage

storage is important. is not impossible to add storage to VM, but it's not easy. give your VMs enough storage space.

  • be prepared for crappy video performance
  • be prepared to sacrifice blur effects and animations in your DE
  • be prepared for mouse buttons not to work extra keys don't work.
  • VMs are not "private by default" your journey for privacy doesn't end there, it is a step, but just not enough.
  • Create yourself a minimal VM A linux that has:
    • Linux installed
    • Just the apps you need
    • Just a browser and your text editor.
  • Block yourself out of distractions
    • Browser extensions and plugin that block you from youtube, cat videos
  • don't forget to customize it, even a little bit unless you really get into it and lose your time.
